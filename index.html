Hi, I’m Ron ^ ^

Welcome to my home on the internet! I’m a research assistant, educator, amateur artist and recent graduate from McMaster University, living in Toronto, Canada. I’m passionate about sharing big ideas and crafting meaningful experiences with audiences across the world.

My background in physiology and programming has taught me to view problems with a multidisciplinary approach that considers optimal trade-offs between various solutions. I’d love to share my interest in biotechnology and health sciences with you. If that sounds fancy, please tune into some of the projects I’ve been working on. Or if you would like to work with me, feel free to shoot me an email. I am always on the lookout for new professional partnerships and engaging opportunities.

In the evening, I enjoy jogging, getting lost in a new book or playing video games with my passionate group of friends. During the course of quarantine, we’ve moved from game to game. Some of us have recently picked up the new and exciting shooter called Valorant. I’m still learning how to aim, but good thing I know where the heart is (because im in physiology lol).

projects

I’m driven to educate and engage with purpose.

Thank you again for tuning into my website. Do you hear that? As we speak, our internet hamsters (essential-workers) are tirelessly scurrying thousands of packets straight to your device.

1. I host a weekly podcast called Here’s the point. Every Sunday, I share some thoughts on a new read, or explore a topic I’ve been pondering.

2. On my YouTube channel, I make videos about health, philosophy, and education.

3. During my undergraduate studies where I spent many of evenings alone in the lab, I explored the beneficial effects of radiation on learning in crickets.

4. The semester before that, we characterized the presence of Allatostatin peptide receptors in the Malpighian tubules of cabbage loopers.
